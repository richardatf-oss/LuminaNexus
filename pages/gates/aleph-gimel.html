GATE · שער

Gate א–ג Aleph → Gimel

From Oneness to Giving

Orientation

Aleph (א) signifies unity, breath, and what precedes speech. Gimel (ג) is classically read as movement toward giving— a “goer” (גומל) who carries benefit outward.

This gate studies how unity expresses itself not as an idea to possess, but as an obligation to move toward the other with restraint and dignity.

אל״ף מסמלת אחדות ונשימה שלפני הדיבור. גימ״ל נקשרת במסורת לתנועה של גמילות חסדים — הליכה אל הזולת. שער זה בודק כיצד אחדות מתגלה לא כהישג, אלא כחובה לפעול ביושר ובצניעות.

Primary Torah Anchors

  • Genesis 12:2
    “...and you shall be a blessing.” In Torah, blessing is not hoarded—it is carried outward.
  • Micah 6:8
    “Do justice, love kindness, and walk humbly…” Giving is bound to humility; action without humility becomes domination.
  • Shabbat 104a (classic letter-teaching)
    Gimel–Dalet is taught as gומל דלים (one who gives to the poor)—the letter itself is remembered as directed generosity.

Boundary

This gate does not authorize “spiritual superiority,” coercion, or turning generosity into a performance.

Aleph is not a badge. Gimel is not control. Giving that pressures is not giving.

שער זה אינו מתיר עליונות רוחנית, כפייה, או “חסד” כהצגה. אל״ף איננה אות כבוד; גימ״ל אינה שליטה. חסד שמפעיל לחץ — אינו חסד.

Chavruta Question

If Aleph is unity, what changes in a person when unity becomes a practice of giving— and how do the sources guard that giving from pride?

Sit with the sources. Do not answer quickly.
