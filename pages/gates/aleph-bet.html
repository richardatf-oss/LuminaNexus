Gate א–ב Aleph → Bet

From Oneness to House

Orientation

Aleph (א) signifies unity, breath, and that which precedes speech. Bet (ב) signifies a house, an interior, a place where something may dwell.

This gate marks the movement from undifferentiated oneness into the first condition that allows meaning: a bounded place.

אל״ף מסמלת אחדות ונשימה שלפני הדיבור. בי״ת מסמלת בית — מקום פנימי שבו דבר יכול לשכון.

Primary Torah Anchors

  • Genesis 1:1 — בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא
    Torah begins with Bet, not Aleph. Oneness precedes the text; the text begins when a house for meaning exists.
  • Bereishit Rabbah
    Bet is closed on three sides and open forward: inquiry begins from creation onward, not before.
  • Sefer Yetzirah 1:1
    Letters act in order. Aleph stands in balance; Bet initiates articulation.

Boundary

This gate does not authorize speculation about pre-creation, secret pronunciations, or personal revelation.

Aleph is real, but not narratable. Bet is narratable, but not ultimate.

האל״ף אמיתית — אך אינה נמסרת בסיפור. הבי״ת נמסרת — אך אינה שלמה.

Chavruta Question

Why does Torah choose to begin with Bet rather than explain Aleph — and what responsibility does that place on the reader?

Sit with the sources. Do not answer quickly.
