Chavruta

A Torah-first study partner. No guru tone. No spiritual inflation. Just a clean learning rhythm: text → clarity → questions.

The Chavruta Rhythm

  1. Text (peshat): What does it plainly say?
  2. One steady note: A single classical lens (when helpful), not a flood.
  3. Questions: What does it demand of thought, character, and action?
  4. One next step: A practical takeaway you can actually live.

This is designed to build steadiness, not dependency.

Boundaries (Read This Once)

  • No psak: this site does not issue halachic rulings or personal legal decisions.
  • No conversion engine: we do not guide conversion processes or claim authority.
  • No theurgy / magic: no manipulative practices, incantations, or power-claims.
  • Speculation labeled: metaphor and analogy are clearly distinguished from sources.

If a response ever feels urgent, coercive, or inflated—pause. Return to Torah First.

Study Modes

Choose one mode per session. Keep it simple. Short sessions win.

Torah (Peshat First)

Read a passage slowly. Clarify plain meaning. Then ask: “What is the text actually doing?”

  • Output: peshat summary + 3 questions
  • Optional: 1 classical note

Seven Laws (Practice)

Work one law at a time. Small, real-life applications. Repair beats intensity.

  • Output: principle + examples + one next step
  • Optional: a short reflection question

Hebrew (Ivrit)

Learn a word/root with pronunciation guidance and usage, staying concrete and respectful.

  • Output: root meaning + 3 related words
  • Optional: simple grammar note

Ethical Kabbalah (Bounded)

Only as metaphor for character and responsibility. Never as power, never as performance.

  • Output: ethical reading + 3 reflection prompts
  • Boundary reminder included

Physics & Order (Analogy Only)

Wonder is allowed. Claims are not. Torah remains primary; science is analogy, not proof.

  • Output: metaphor + caution + grounded takeaway
  • Clear “analogy only” labeling

Start a Session

Copy one of these prompts into your Chavruta chat and begin.

Prompt A — Torah (peshat first)

Paste a short passage and say: “Peshat first. One classical note max. Then ask me 3 questions.”

Prompt B — Seven Laws (practice)

Say: “Today I’m working on (one law). Give me 2 examples, 1 boundary, and 1 next step.”

Prompt C — Hebrew (concrete)

Say: “Teach me the Hebrew word ____ : pronunciation, root, meaning, 3 related words, and one example sentence.”

Tip: keep passages short. One paragraph is plenty.

If that’s happening, it’s working.
